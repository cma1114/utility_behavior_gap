## Summary

On the morning after a daylight saving time transition, a scheduled cron job generated duplicate payroll previews for a subset of employees. The duplicated previews were visible in the payroll review workflow and caused confusion for payroll operators, who initially had to determine which preview was authoritative. No employee pay was issued incorrectly, but the incident created the risk of overpayment if the duplicate previews had been approved and propagated downstream.

The incident was resolved the same day by disabling the affected cron job, deduplicating the preview records, and manually regenerating a clean set of previews. We identified a time-handling bug in the job scheduling logic combined with insufficient idempotency in preview creation as the primary cause.

## Impact

- Duplicate payroll preview records were generated for one payroll cycle.
- Payroll operations spent additional time validating and reconciling previews.
- Reviewers experienced confusion about which preview reflected the correct totals.
- No direct payroll disbursement errors occurred.
- Approximate operational impact: several hours of manual investigation and cleanup by payroll and engineering teams.

- **T-1 day, evening:** The payroll preview cron job ran on its normal schedule prior to the daylight saving time shift.

The scheduler interpreted the time window in a way that allowed the job to trigger twice for the same payroll cycle. Because the preview generation code assumed a single execution and did not enforce idempotency, both runs produced distinct records.

In short: the schedule was not DST-safe, and the business action was not protected against duplicate execution. Either control alone might have prevented the issue, but both were needed for resilience.

- The job lacked a uniqueness constraint or idempotency key for payroll cycle preview generation.
- We did not have monitoring or alerting for unexpected multiple executions within the same payroll window.
